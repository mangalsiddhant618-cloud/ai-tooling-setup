# 47.5% of scrolling on AIO SERPs goes backward
**Kevin Indig** · [View post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_475-of-all-scrolling-on-aio-serps-goes-activity-7465722306871439360-q12R)

## Post text
47.5% of all scrolling on AIO SERPs goes backward, compared to 27% without an AIO on the page.

Among users who reverse direction on an AIO SERP, the median user spends nearly half their total scroll going back up the page.

Scrolling stopped being a one-way trip. On an AIO SERP, the user reads, weighs, scrolls past, then circles back to re-read.

The implication for meta descriptions: your listing is no longer getting one impression. It's getting 2, sometimes 3. The first impression earns the read on the way down. The second impression earns the click on the way back up.

The tactical implication: think about how to make your meta title and description scroll-stopping without sacrificing trust.

## Why this matters
A behavioral data point with a direct content-production consequence. Because AIO
SERPs make users scroll back up and re-read, a single listing now earns 2–3
impressions instead of one. So meta titles/descriptions should be written to be
"scroll-stopping" on the way back up — not just keyword-stuffed — while keeping trust
intact. Reframes meta copy as a multi-impression asset, not a one-shot snippet.
