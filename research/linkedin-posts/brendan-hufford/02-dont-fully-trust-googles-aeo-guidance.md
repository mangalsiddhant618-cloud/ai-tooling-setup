# "Google says AEO/GEO is still SEO." I don't believe them.
**Brendan Hufford** · [View post](https://www.linkedin.com/in/brendanhufford/recent-activity/all/)

## Post text
Breaking: Google just published an official guide saying AEO and GEO are "still SEO."

They also said you don't need llms.txt files, chunking, or AI-specific schema.

They said just keep doing SEO (aka the thing they said not to do for a decade+)

(Dis)respectfully: I don't believe them.

And neither should you.

Google has a pattern of saying one thing publicly while their actual systems reward the opposite. We've seen this movie before:

1. Google said Chrome data wasn’t used in ranking websites.

Antitrust trial confirmed it's a direct ranking signal.

2. Google said to "avoid link building."

Antitrust trial explicitly confirmed its a key quality signal and anchor text is a "valuable clue" for relevance.

Look - Google's public advice always protects THEIR interests.

I've been tracking 100+ AEO prompts per client engagement for over a year.

What I'm seeing in the data doesn't match what Google is telling us.

Does this mean ignore everything they say? No.

But treat their guidance as one data point, not gospel.

⚡️

PS - GREAT content marketing means your can win in both SEO *and* AEO. You just need the right strategy behind it.
