# Newsletters & articles — annotated index

Links to the strongest recent *written* work from the 10 experts, with a one-line note
on why it's worth reading for an AI-content-production playbook. (Links + takeaways
only — full articles are not reproduced here, to respect copyright.)

---

## Kevin Indig — Growth Memo
- [GSC data is 75% incomplete](https://www.growth-memo.com/p/gsc-data-is-75-incomplete) —
  original analysis of ~10 B2B SaaS sites (~4M clicks); why your measurement baseline
  is probably wrong before you even start producing content.
- [How Google's AI Overviews work](https://www.growth-memo.com/p/ai-on-innovation) —
  the largest AIO analysis cited (546k rows); what actually gets cited.
- [Growth Intelligence Briefs](https://www.growth-memo.com/) — running tracker of
  AI-visibility vs. SEO-visibility trends across thousands of domains.
- **Why it matters:** measurement + data discipline. The antidote to "publish more AI
  pages and hope."

## Jake Ward — Byword / LinkedIn
- ["World's largest AI SEO case study"](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jakezward) —
  ~1M monthly organic from ~7,000 AI-generated pages; the canonical scale case.
- **Why it matters:** the most concrete public example of AI content production *at
  volume* — and the QA/risk lessons that come with it.

## Eli Schwartz — Product-Led SEO
- [Rethinking SEO in the age of AI (Lenny's Newsletter)](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/rethinking-seo-in-the-age-of-ai-eli-schwartz) —
  shift from keyword/top-of-funnel to user-journey + mid-funnel intent.
- [elischwartz.co](https://www.elischwartz.co/) — product-led SEO essays.
- **Why it matters:** the strategic frame — treat content/SEO as a product problem, not
  a volume problem. Keeps AI production pointed at intent, not just pages.

## Cyrus Shepard — Zyppy
- [Zyppy Signal (Substack)](https://signal.zyppy.com/) — experiment-driven findings.
- Internal-links study: 23M internal links analyzed → 7 internal-linking rules
  ([Zyppy SEO blog](https://zyppy.com/seo/)).
- **Why it matters:** the "test it, don't assume it" discipline. Internal linking is the
  highest-leverage lever when you produce content at scale.
