# Practitioner vs. packaging — a deliberate contrast case

The brief warned against people who "just write about it" rather than practice. A subtler
trap is the opposite: someone who **genuinely practices** but wraps it in marketing that's
indistinguishable from a grifter. I kept one such case out of the main 10 on purpose, and
documented it here, because telling these apart *is* the skill being tested.

## Case: Julian Goldie

**The real operator side (credible):**
- Founder of Goldie Agency — a real SEO/link-building agency (reportedly 50+ staff,
  100+ clients).
- Builds and shares working **n8n automation workflows** for AI content production live.
- 77K+ YouTube subscribers, 128K+ on X; clearly produces and ships.

**The packaging side (red flags):**
- Titles like *"AI Video Automation Made Me $10K This Month (While I Slept)"*,
  *"$1M+ Business Creating 600+ Pieces of Content Daily"*, *"Million-Dollar Automations
  While You Sleep."*
- Heavy funnel into a paid Skool community ("AI Profit Boardroom"), courses, and
  templates — the income often comes from *selling the method*, not only from using it.

## Why this matters for the project

If you're building a real playbook, you can still **mine the workflows** (the n8n
automations are genuinely useful) — but you have to **discount the claims** and ignore the
income-promise framing. The judgment call: *take the mechanics, leave the marketing.*

This is exactly why the main-list selection weighted **published data, named clients, and
controlled experiments** over follower counts and headline claims.
